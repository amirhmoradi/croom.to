Why Choose Croom?

In a world where video conferencing has become critical infrastructure, organizations deserve control over their communication systems.

The Problem with Enterprise Video Conferencing

Professional video conferencing hardware has remained inaccessible to most organizations. Here's what we're fighting against:

Outrageous Prices

$3,000-15,000 per room plus ongoing per-seat licensing. A 10-room deployment costs $30,000+ in year one alone.

Vendor Lock-in

Proprietary systems force dependency on single vendors. Switching costs are designed to trap you.

Privacy Concerns

Cloud processing means your video and audio data passes through third-party servers you don't control.

Opaque Systems

No visibility into how the software works. You can't audit, customize, or understand what's running in your meeting rooms.

Our Mission

We believe professional video conferencing should be accessible to everyone — schools, NGOs, small businesses, community organizations, and enterprises alike.

Croom was created to democratize video conferencing technology. By leveraging affordable hardware like Raspberry Pi and open-source software, we've made enterprise-grade features available at a fraction of the cost.

Digital Resiliency & Data Privacy

Your meetings, your data, your infrastructure. No compromises.

Self-Hosted Infrastructure

All processing happens on YOUR hardware. Deploy on-premise or in your own cloud. No external dependencies required.

Privacy by Design

AI features run locally via Hailo or Coral accelerators. No video or audio data ever leaves your network for processing.

Open Source Transparency

MIT licensed. Inspect, audit, modify every line of code. Know exactly what's running in your meeting rooms.

Vendor Independence

Works with Google Meet, Teams, Zoom, Webex. Switch platforms freely without hardware changes.

European Values

GDPR-ready architecture. Built with European privacy regulations in mind. A French initiative for global benefit.

Air-Gapped Ready

Operate completely offline in secure environments. Perfect for government, military, and high-security organizations.

The Open Source Advantage

Open source isn't just a license — it's a commitment to transparency, community, and long-term sustainability.

Resilience & Continuity

Proprietary vendors can be acquired, pivot strategy, or discontinue products. With Croom, the source code is public and MIT licensed forever. Even if the core team disbands, anyone can fork and continue development.

  • No vendor discontinuation risk
  • Community-driven maintenance
  • Fork and customize as needed

Security Through Transparency

Security through obscurity doesn't work. With open source, anyone can audit the code, find vulnerabilities, and contribute fixes. The community acts as thousands of security researchers.

  • Public security audits
  • Rapid vulnerability disclosure
  • Community-contributed fixes

No Per-Seat Pricing

Deploy to unlimited rooms. No per-device fees that scale against you. Whether you have 1 room or 1,000, the software is free forever.

  • Free forever (MIT license)
  • Unlimited devices
  • Predictable hardware-only costs

Customization & Integration

Need a specific feature or integration? With open source, you can build it yourself or hire anyone to do it. No waiting for vendor roadmaps.

  • Full access to source code
  • Build custom integrations
  • No vendor dependency
