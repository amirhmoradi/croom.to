About Croom

Open-source video conferencing for the rest of us.

The Problem

Enterprise video conferencing hardware is absurdly expensive. A single Cisco Webex Room Kit can cost $5,000-15,000. For an organization with 50 meeting rooms, that's $250,000-750,000 just for hardware, plus ongoing licensing fees.

Meanwhile, the Raspberry Pi in your desk drawer has more computing power than these "enterprise" devices, costs $75, and can run Linux.

The Solution

Croom turns affordable hardware into professional video conferencing systems. Using a Raspberry Pi, a decent USB camera, and optional AI accelerators, you get:

  • Support for all major platforms (Google Meet, Teams, Zoom, Webex)
  • AI-powered auto-framing and speaker tracking
  • Calendar integration with auto-join
  • Touch screen room control
  • Fleet management for hundreds of rooms
  • Meeting transcription and AI summaries

All for about $300-500 per room, with no recurring fees for the Community edition.

Open Source Philosophy

Croom is released under the MIT license. This means:

  • Free forever - The core platform will always be free
  • No vendor lock-in - You own your infrastructure
  • Community-driven - Features shaped by actual users
  • Transparency - Audit the code yourself
  • Self-hosted - Your data stays on your servers
